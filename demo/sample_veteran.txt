Harold Dean Whitaker, age 82, of Gallatin, Missouri, passed away Saturday evening, March 8, 2026, at his home surrounded by family. Harold was born February 14, 1944, in Gallatin, the son of Raymond and Dorothy (Sims) Whitaker. He graduated from Gallatin High School in 1962 and enlisted in the United States Army, serving two tours in Vietnam with the 25th Infantry Division. He was awarded the Bronze Star and Purple Heart. On June 5, 1966, he married Patricia Ann Miller in Gallatin. She survives of the home. Harold worked as a heavy equipment operator for MoDOT for 32 years, retiring in 1998. He was a member of First Baptist Church of Gallatin, the VFW Post 3108, and the Gallatin Masonic Lodge. He loved fishing at Smithville Lake, woodworking, and spoiling his grandchildren. Harold is survived by his wife, Patricia; four children, Steven Whitaker and wife Karen of Gallatin, Michael Whitaker and wife Donna of Cameron, Brenda Carlisle and husband Jim of Chillicothe, and Lisa Morgan and husband David of Kansas City; twelve grandchildren; eight great-grandchildren; and a sister, Carolyn Foster of Trenton. He was preceded in death by his parents; a brother, Kenneth Whitaker; and a grandson, Tyler Carlisle. Funeral services will be held at 10:00 a.m. Wednesday, March 12, 2026, at First Baptist Church with Pastor Bill Henderson officiating. Burial with military honors will follow at the Missouri Veterans Cemetery in Higginsville, Missouri. Visitation will be Tuesday from 6:00 to 8:00 p.m. at Stith Funeral Home in Gallatin. Memorial contributions may be made to the VFW Post 3108 Scholarship Fund.
